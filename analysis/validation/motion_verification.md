# Motion Verification
## Objective
In order to confirm that the designed gearbox behaved as expected, a SolidWorks motion study was performed. This study was
intended to verify that shaft rotation, gear meshing and the overall gearbox reduction ratio behaved as expected.

## Simulation Setup
The gearbox assembly was configured using SolidWorks gear mates to define the appropriate gear ratio between meshing gears. 
A motor input was applied to the input shaft to simulate rotational motion. The assembly was set up to allow rotation of shafts,
whilst gears were fixed to their shafts.

## Motion Behaviour
When the input shaft was rotated all three shafts rotated as expected. Each gear pair rotated according to the defined gear 
mate relationships and the intermediate and output shafts rotated smoothly without any interference.

The motion study confirmed that all components were properly constrained and that the assembly behaves as intended.

## Gear Ratio Verification
The motion simulation confirmed the expected gear reduction behaviour.

Stage 1:
12T gear → 60T gear = 5:1

Stage 2:
15T gear → 60T gear = 4:1

Total gearbox reduction:

5 × 4 = 20:1

This means the output shaft rotates 20 times slower than the input shaft and with 20 times the torque.

## Conclusion
The motion study verified that the gearbox assembly transmits motion correctly through both gear stages and produces the 
intended 20:1 reduction ratio. This confirms that the gear mates, assembly constraints, and and positioing of gears and shafts
is acceptable.
