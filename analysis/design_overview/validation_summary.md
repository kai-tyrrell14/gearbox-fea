# Validation Summary

## Design Objective
The objective this project was to design and validate a small two-stage gearbox capable of increasing torque, reducing speed and useful for robotic actuation applications.

## Kinematic Validation
A SolidWorks motion study was performed to verify the correct transmission of motion through both gear stages, with gear mates used to ensure tooth relationships and a motor input applied to the input shaft.

The simulation verified smooth rotation of all shafts and the expected gearbox reduction ratio of 20:1.

## Structural Validation
A Finite Element Analysis was perfomred to evaluate stress and displacement behaviour under an applied load.

With maximum stress occuring at gear tooth roots, at these points of the FEA stress results, stress levels were below the assumed material yield limits.

Similarly, the displacement results showed minimal deformation indicating the gear meshing would remain reliable during operation.

## Engineering Verification

Design calculations and modelling decisions were consistent throughout development:

- Gear ratios matched calculations.
- Shaft diameters increased appropriately as the torque transmitted increased.
- Appropriate deep-groove ball bearings were used for expected radial loads.
- Housing geometry ensured sufficient component alignment and support.

## Conclusion
The conceptual design of the gearbox has been successfully validated. Kinematic and structural validations demonstrated that the gearbox is suitable for its application in small robotic drivetrains. In future, fatigue and efficiency analyses could be undertaken in addition to the manufacturing of a physical prototype.
