# FEA Results
## Objective
A Finite Element Analysis was carried out on the output spur gear to evaluate the bending stress at the gear's tooth root 
and displacement under torque loading. This was critical in order to verify the gearbox's suitability for the applications intended.

## Stress Results
The maximum von Mises stress occurred at the loaded tooth's root fillet, which is the expected critical region for bending stress
in a spur gear. This confirms that the simulation setup produced realistic results. In addition, peak stress remained well below the 
material yield strength which indicates that the selected gear dimensions are appropriate for the intended loading condition.

## Displacement Results
Maximum displacement occurred near the loaded tooth tip, whilst closer to the bore was effectively constrained. This is 
consistent with expected bending behaviour of a loaded gear tooth. The displacement magnitude was relatively small which
indicates that gear deformation would have minimal impact on meshing performance under the analysed load case.

## Engineering Interpretation
The results support the under the expected loading conditions the gearbox is suitable for the intended applications. Both the 
stress distribution and displacement results aligned with the expected results, and no abnormal deformation or 
unrealistic stress concentrations were observed.
